## Overview
Basic medication information for common drugs found in survival kits and pharmacies.

## Step-by-step
1. Ibuprofen: 200-400mg every 4-6h for pain/fever/inflammation (max 1200mg/day OTC).
2. Acetaminophen: 500-1000mg every 4-6h for pain/fever (max 3000mg/day; liver toxic in overdose).
3. Diphenhydramine (Benadryl): 25-50mg every 6h for allergic reactions, sleep aid.
4. Loperamide (Imodium): 4mg initially, then 2mg after each loose stool (max 16mg/day).
5. Oral antibiotics (if available): amoxicillin 500mg 3x/day for general infections.
6. Always check for allergies before administering any medication.

## Warnings
- This is emergency reference only — not a substitute for medical training.
- Acetaminophen overdose causes fatal liver failure — never exceed dose.
- Drug interactions and allergies can be deadly — document what is given and when.
- Pediatric doses differ significantly — do not give adult doses to children.
