## Overview
Dual-purpose plant providing both nutritious greens and protein-rich grain. Thrives in poor soils and heat. One of the most calorie-dense wild foods.

## Step-by-step
1. Identify by alternate simple leaves, dense flower spikes (often red/purple), tiny seeds.
2. Harvest leaves when young for cooking like spinach.
3. Collect seed heads when dry; thresh and winnow seeds.
4. Seeds: toast lightly or grind into flour. High in lysine (rare in grains).
5. Boil greens briefly to reduce oxalates.
